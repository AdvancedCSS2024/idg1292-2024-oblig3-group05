Sweep The Sea

How big of a problem is this?

Plastic pollution in the ocean is a massive environmental issue. Each year, about eight million tons of plastic end up in the ocean, threatening marine life through ingestion and entanglement, and introducing toxic chemicals into food chains. This impacts economically important industries like fishing and tourism and necessitates costly cleanup efforts. Microplastics, pervasive throughout the ocean, pose a continuous threat as they can be ingested by marine organisms and eventually humans. Addressing this pressing problem requires global cooperation and innovations in waste management.

Why should you care?

Caring about plastic pollution in the ocean is vital because it directly impacts marine ecosystems and the global environment. The ingestion and entanglement of wildlife with plastic lead to biodiversity loss and disrupt marine habitats. Furthermore, plastics break down into microplastics, contaminating the food chain, potentially affecting human health. Economic sectors like fishing and tourism suffer, leading to financial losses globally. Addressing plastic pollution is essential for preserving ocean health, safeguarding food security, and ensuring sustainable economic activities.
